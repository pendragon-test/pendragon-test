Things In Motion

The texture of the batter is getting pretty thick, almost ready to bake. After a while, it's time for me to dump the batter into two pans and pre-heat the oven. Sounds of an audiobook plays through Bluetooth speakers. Connection is spotty, so it skips around, and I'm frequently rewinding on my device so I don’t miss stuff. The date of August 20th is circled on the calendar. The strong scent of cake fills the kitchen. Humming Cyndi Lauper’s “Time After Time”, I carefully spread the icing, cutting six strawberries in half and place the 12 halves equidistant along the circumference.
 The cake fits nicely in the fridge. Just 4 more days until her birthday. The day ends with the air of accomplishment. I go to bed satisfied and feel all set for next Thursday. The next day; I leave my apartment and walk out to the college campus next door. Then I make my way into room 142: Aerospace Engineering. Jessica and Maki are already in their seats. Since there’s still time, I sit down with the two.
“At some point you have to grow up. You know I’m not gonna be around forever.”
“I don’t feel comfortable doing it; I feel bothersome taking time out of people's days. "
“What are you talking about?” Malcolm interludes.
“Jessica, again. She’s too afraid to ask Calvin to go bowling with us.” Maki turned to me to answer and back to Jessica. “Look, sometimes you just need to be selfish and let people know you wanna be with them.”
The class starts. I get to my seat and prepare my laptop for the lesson. The teacher surprises us with a quiz on zeppelins and their current use today. I think I got a decent grade on it. It’s just three days away from Maki's birthday. I wanna make my feelings for her stand out more than just fancy food. The day ends, and I head back to my apartment, stewing over ideas of what more I could do for her birthday. 
The next day comes, and it’s now 2 more days before her big day. I know she likes planes, so maybe we can go to an aerospace museum? I’ll try to see which ones are close by. I take out my phone and look at my physical calendar while looking up museums. There’s a date for another test scheduled for next Wednesday written on the wall calendar. “Great, a gauntlet”, I mutter. 
Leaving my apartment, I came across Jessica slowly making her way down the street. Hopefully, she contacts Calvin soon. It’d be fun to have a 4th player. I walk into the classroom and set up. During class, Jessica arrives a few minutes late. We learn more about dirigibles, and at the end of class Jessica, Maki, and I go over our test results.
Maki excitedly exclaims, “I got 100” and hugs us.
“Of course you passed, you’re a big old nerd when it comes to this stuff.” Jessica said, trying to suppress a frown.
“Something wrong? Did you not pass?” I ask.
“No, I asked Calvin, and he said he already has plans with his boyfriend.
“Well, I guess it’s just the three of us then. More pizza for us,” Maki smiles. 
The day ends, and tomorrow starts. Maki’s birthday is tomorrow. I made all the arrangements and made sure the cake was still good. I make my way to class knowing that Maki will have the best day of her life. After getting to class, I prepare my stuff before the teacher gets in. I hear Maki and Jessica chattering outside as they get closer to the classroom.
"I can't wait, Malcolm told me he was gonna make me something special. He's such a marvelous cook."
"What if it isn't food?" Jessica asked.
"It's food. He always has something delicious planned for any occasion, though sometimes he's a day off."
"When is your birthday again, Maki? I forgot."
"The 20th, so in nine days."
Nine days echo in my head. In nine days; the cake will go bad in 6, maybe 7 days. The strawberries will rot. I know I'm bad with dates, but an entire week? I get out of my chair and head to the bathroom, passing the two girls. I walk down the hall and take a left into the bathroom. The room is covered in a starry sky. In the center of the room, there's a cherry wood desk covered in hourglasses. I can't help but feel I recognize one of the hourglasses on the table; it has very little sand left. 
A young man, about 25, is sitting behind the desk. He has a white goatee; out-dated fashion, and a pair of scissors where one blade is drastically shorter than the other.
"Where-?" I turn around and there's no door.
An elderly voice comes from his mouth, "I'm here to grant you a redo of the events of last Sunday."
The impossibility of this situation requires me to believe him, "You can actually have it so the ingredients I used don't go to waste?" 
"Of course I can. I’m Father Time and I can shape events so you can get the outcome you desire."
My bedroom door appears behind me, and I enter it. I walk out and I'm in the hall of my apartment. 
"This is last Sunday. If you close the door, you will lock yourself into this new timeline, and this door will lead back to your bedchamber. "
 I check the pantry and fridge; all the ingredients I used are back in place. There are still eggs and butter remaining. Father Time is right; this is Sunday, before I made the cake. 
"Thank you." I say as I close the door. I sit down and watch some anime, then go to bed, deciding not to bake anything.
The rest of the week plays out the same as they did the first time I experienced it. I no longer need to buy groceries since I didn't use them up on Sunday. The aerospace professor wraps up his class. After packing up my laptop, I go down to the student lounge to get some food. I see Calvin sitting by himself. I go up and sit next to him since he’s a familiar face, and it’s unnerving eating alone.
“So you have any big plans for the weekend?” I ask 
“Not anymore. I just broke up with my boyfriend, and I had the perfect restaurant picked out too, but now I had to cancel because of the breakup. What about you?”
“The girls and I were gonna go bowling.” I thought Jessica said she asked him? 
“Oh cool, can I tag along?”
“Sure, I’ll let the girls know next time I see them.” We wrap up our conversation; I finish my meal, and we split off. 
I head to my apartment, all excited for the weekend. My outfit has already been planned, which includes my coolest buttoned down shirt. I go to bed excited for the weekend. The next day after class, I overhear Maki goading Jessica into doing something again. It’s painfully clear who the leader of the friend group is. Oh, I guess it’s my turn. Jessica nervously walks toward me. “Do you want to study with us? I’m sorry if it takes time out of your day, but I could really use the help.”
“Sure, I’m all done with errands, so I have some time to kill”
Maki gives a thumbs-up.
“That’s great.”
We walk down the hall single file; Jesica in the front, me in the middle, and Maki in the back. I hear a thud behind me. Me and Jesica turn around and Maki is lying on the ground.
I take out my phone and call campus emergency services; Jesica rushes to Maki's aid. E.M.T.s soon arrive. Maki is too out of it to respond and she’s loaded onto a stretcher, immediately taking her to the hospital. 
“Hey, Jessica, can you keep me company while we wait for an answer? I know you can always text me any information, but i-”
“Sure, I can keep you company”.
Me and Jessica wait in my apartment w. Watching some slice of life anime while waiting for a response. Four episodes in, Jessica, as an emergency contact, finally gets a response.
"Yes, I'm here. Is Maki alright? Oh, thank God. Surgery? You contacted her parents? Oh good. Thanks for keeping me updated." Jesica turns to me and gets up. 
"What did they say? Is she okay?"
"Yeah, she just needs heart surgery."
"That's good, hope we can visit soon."
"We will. I'm, I’m heading out back to my place. Thanks for being with us, even though we didn’t do any studying." Jesica leaves. 
I go to my bedroom and get some sleep. I'm glad you were here too. The next few days were silent. Maki is going into surgery prep for an artificial heart. We can visit her after the procedure. For now, I just have to focus on my schoolwork and pray for the best. These days are the longest I've ever experienced. The third day after class, Jessica waits for me in the hall.
"Malcolm, wanna go to the hospital to visit Maki?"
"She's already done with surgery?"
"Yup, got the okay, and everything. Let's go."
I nod my head, and we head over immediately. The hospital is a short bus ride from campus. We go past a park, a few restaurants, and arrive at the building. The lady at check-in gave us the room number Maki's in. An I.V. is flowing into her left hand, a 24-hour channel of How it’s Made is on the T.V., and the worst part about this scene is the hospital food. I step forward but stop myself from hugging her.
"I'm so relieved you're okay; I was worried when we had to call emergency services."
"Yeah, I'm okay. I should be golden in a few days, and I can have some food that actually tastes good."
"I'll make you a feast once you're out of here."
"Hopefully, we can do something fun when I get out; being this stationary is awful."
Time goes by rather fast, and before we know it, visiting hours are over. We spend each day after class visiting Maki. She looks as if her condition is improving gradually. The doctors want to keep her a little longer. That room made it look like it's easy to lose track of time; fluorescent lights constantly on, How it’s Made all hours of the day, and not a single window. I bet the only time she gets a good idea of what time it is when we visit. She's probably too weak to even use her phone.
It’s now August 17th. After class, I go down to the student lounge to get something to eat. I sit down with my meal, and then Calvin plops down next to me as I’m about to take my first bite.
“What happened last week? I thought you were gonna ask the girls if I could go bowling with them.”
“Sorry, we had to cancel; Maki had to go to the hospital.”
“Oh,-that sucks. -You should’ve told me sooner. I really care about you guys.”
“Thanks, she should be out soon. Maybe we can reschedule. Sorry for not telling you, I don’t really know how to contact you other than via Jessica.”
We finish our meals and again head off in our separate ways. This time I ask Calvin for his number. I also tell him the room number and the hospital Maki is staying at. It's now August 18th, and Maki is looking worse. Her doctors told her that her new heart is already failing her. I just sit and watch How It’s Made, with her, holding her free hand. After class on the 19th, I go down to the student lounge with Jessica, and we play pool before our daily visit to the hospital.
"This is sad." I say.
"Well, we are her friends; we knew this would happen at some point." 
"Yeah, but to spend your birthday in the hospital."
"Why haven't you confessed to her yet?"
"What?"
"You always made extravagant meals, and instead of culinary arts, you took aerospace."
"well-"
"You're the type I don't want to see worried, so do it tomorrow; confess to her."
"Okay."
“Can I confess something to you?”
“Sure” 
Jessica prepares herself for a long speech. “I saw Father Time after Calvin admitted he already had plans when we planned for bowling. I went back in time that day so that I never asked him. I felt guilty for inviting him when he already had plans. But then you asked me to stay with you when Maki got rushed to the hospital, and that put things in perspective. That wasn’t something I should feel guilty about; I shouldn’t beat myself up for taking time out of someone's day, cause what if they’re feeling the same way?”
“If Maki was here, she’d slap you with a paper fan. I see what you mean by confessing to her, though.” I’d think she was nuts if I didn't see Father Time myself.
“Also, there was something in there that scared me. It was an hourglass being real close to running out of sand. I don’t know why, but this particular one struck me. Like, what would I do if it ran out. How would I act if it was no longer there to encourage me?
It’s the 20th, and Maki is the worst she’s ever been.‌ Disheveled hair, a barely touched lunch. It strikes me, this may be the last time I ever see her.
"Maki, I."
"I'm glad you're here. I would've loved to have your cooking one last time."
"I would always be glad to cook for you; I always enjoyed watching you eat my meals."
“And I always enjoyed eating your meals.” Maki smiles and closes her eyes.
“Maki, I love you.”
6:30 a 25-year-old man, with a goatee and scissors where the blades are two different lengths, walks in. I recognize the hourglass with very little sand left, now with only 5 grains left.
"Do you understand now?"
"I think I get it." I pull the museum tickets from my pocket. “The day doesn’t really matter, cause I can’t count on tomorrow. The bank may be closed, but I could take a picture of a frog on my way there.”
He cuts a thread from Maki's head and dies August 20th, 6:30 with a smile.
